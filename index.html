About

About ...

Computer programming veteran since the days of the Apple IIe. Passionate about games, children’s education, and AI — especially where it intersects with psychology and neuroscience. Visual by nature and big-picture oriented, with a strong interest in making complex ideas useful, clear, and engaging.

Currently focused on the coding education. Able to design and build custom teaching plans from the ground up, with a focus on helping students learn through hands-on, project-based work. Has created original tools that let students learn coding by playing mini JavaScript games — like navigating a character through a maze using code to collect and return items. Believes Roblox is a strong platform for teaching kids to code and enjoys incorporating it into learning when possible. Also built a full open-world RPG game from scratch in Roblox. Has a good sense of when and where students get stuck and adjusts the approach to help things click naturally. Works well with companies or schools looking for creative, practical ways to teach tech, and for someone who is good with kids and students.

Has taught STEM-focused computer classes in various high schools, including classes for a school architecture design program where my students placed 1st and 2nd in competition.

Most days end with a good film and some quiet time working on my personal game project.
